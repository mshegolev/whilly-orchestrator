<objective>
Prove the semantic drift-detection engine is trustworthy, not merely plausible: build
a known-drift fixture (a deliberately drifted spec/code pair) plus a clean control
pair, and validate the engine flags the drifted case HIGH while reporting the control
clean (VALID-01).

Validation lands at TWO layers per the locked CONTEXT decision:
1. A DETERMINISTIC offline plumbing test (TDD core) feeds each fixture through the real
   `review_spec` pipeline with a SCRIPTED reviewer (HIGH for drifted, `[]` for control)
   and asserts the harness correctly classifies detected-HIGH vs clean. Always CI-green
   offline (no network, no CLI).
2. A LIVE acceptance canary uses the REAL `claude_reviewer`, skips when
   `shutil.which("claude")` is None, and asserts >=1 HIGH on the drifted fixture AND
   zero HIGH on the control. Asserts SEVERITY-level outcome only — never on the LLM's
   exact wording.

Purpose: VALID-01 — the guard ships demonstrably trustworthy before v1.5 closes.
Output: self-contained fixtures under tests/fixtures/semantic_drift/{drifted,clean}/,
a fixture README, and a two-layer test module. This is the FINAL v1.5 phase: zero
whilly/ change, no gating change.
</objective>
